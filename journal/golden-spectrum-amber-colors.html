The Golden Spectrum: Understanding Amber Colors and Their Meaning

October 2025
← Back to Journal

Amber is not a single color. It is a universe — a spectrum that ranges from the palest champagne to the deepest black, shaped over tens of millions of years by the chemistry of ancient forests. To hold an AMBERRA piece is to hold geology made visible: the hue you see was determined by the composition of long-extinct resin, the minerals in the soil where the tree once stood, and the precise conditions under which that resin was slowly transformed into stone. Understanding these colors is, in a very real sense, understanding the history of the earth itself.

At AMBERRA, our artisans in Ubud select every piece of raw Balinese amber individually, reading its color, clarity, and internal landscape before placing it in silver or gold-plated settings. The color of amber is not merely aesthetic — it carries distinct energetic and geological signatures. Here, we explore the six primary varieties you will encounter across our collection.

Honey and Golden Amber

The most widely recognized amber variety, honey and golden amber ranges from pale straw yellow to a rich warm gold that seems to hold sunlight inside it. This coloration results from a relatively pure resin composition with minimal organic inclusions — the ancient tree sap polymerized cleanly, with few impurities to darken or shift the tone. When light passes through a piece of honey amber, it illuminates the stone from within, producing the warm luminosity that has made amber prized for thousands of years.

Energetically, honey and golden amber is associated with warmth, vitality, and optimism. Many Balinese spiritual traditions regard golden amber as a carrier of solar energy — the concentrated essence of light stored within the earth. It is considered supportive for clarity of mind and upliftment of mood, making it one of the most versatile varieties for daily wear.

Cognac Amber

Cognac amber occupies the warmer, deeper end of the golden spectrum — a reddish-brown that recalls the aged brandy for which it is named. The color comes primarily from the presence of iron oxides and other mineral compounds that seeped into the resin over millennia, as well as from natural oxidation occurring near the surface of ancient amber deposits. The longer amber is exposed to oxygen and pressure within its geological layer, the deeper and more complex its cognac tones become.

Spiritually and energetically, cognac amber is prized for its grounding qualities. Where golden amber lifts, cognac amber anchors. It is associated with stability, rootedness, and a sense of deep calm — the quality of an ancient tree itself, patient and immovable. AMBERRA artisans frequently select cognac pieces for statement rings and cuff bracelets, where the commanding depth of color suits the boldness of the form.

Cherry Amber

Cherry amber — ranging from translucent burgundy to a vivid red-orange — is among the most visually striking varieties. Its color derives from high concentrations of succinic acid combined with specific organic inclusions, particularly from certain species of coniferous trees whose resin chemistry was naturally more resinous and phenolic. Heat exposure during the geological formation process can also deepen amber's color toward the cherry and red range.

Cherry amber carries passionate, activating energy. In various amber-working traditions, red-toned pieces are associated with courage, life force, and protection. The color itself speaks of fire — dynamic, transformative, alive. In the AMBERRA collection, cherry amber pendants and earrings are often chosen as focal pieces: a single stone, set simply in sterling silver bezel, allowed to speak entirely for itself.

Every color of amber is a chapter in an unwritten book — millions of years of chemistry, pressure, and transformation made visible in a stone small enough to rest at your throat.

Green Amber

Representing roughly one percent of all amber found worldwide, green amber is among the rarest and most sought-after varieties. Its unusual coloration results from the presence of plant matter — specifically chlorophyll from mosses, ferns, or other vegetation — that was trapped and preserved within the resin as it polymerized. In some cases, pyrite inclusions or unique mineral interactions contribute additional green or teal tones. The color is often most visible when held to natural daylight; in artificial light, some green amber can appear more golden or olive.

Given its rarity, green amber holds a special place in AMBERRA's curation process. When a green piece is sourced, it receives particular attention from our Ubud artisans, who design a setting that honors the stone's natural form as much as possible. Energetically, green amber is strongly associated with healing, renewal, and connection to nature — qualities that feel especially resonant in a stone formed within ancient living forests.

Milky White and Butterscotch Amber

Milky or butterscotch amber has an opaque or semi-opaque appearance quite unlike the transparency of its more common cousins. This cloudiness is caused by microscopic air bubbles — sometimes thousands per cubic millimeter — that became trapped within the resin during the polymerization process. A single cubic centimeter of butterscotch amber can contain up to 900,000 tiny air inclusions, each one scattering light and creating that characteristic soft, creamy glow.

This variety is deeply associated with calming, soothing energy. The softness of its appearance seems to extend into its felt quality: where translucent amber focuses and concentrates energy, butterscotch amber diffuses it, creating a sense of gentle warmth and ease. AMBERRA uses butterscotch pieces particularly in designs intended for everyday wear — pieces meant to accompany you quietly through the ordinary hours of the day.

Black Amber

Black amber is exceptional in every sense. Deeply rare and found in only a handful of deposits globally, its color comes from extremely high concentrations of organic matter — plant debris, peat, even preserved insect populations — that were incorporated into the resin in such volume that they rendered it nearly opaque and dark. In some specimens, the black coloration results from thick layers of pyrite or other metalite minerals forming around the amber over geological time.

In spiritual traditions, black amber is universally considered highly protective. It is thought to absorb and neutralize negative energies, functioning as a kind of energetic shield for the wearer. AMBERRA sources black amber very selectively, reserving it for a small number of pieces each season. When set in 24k gold-plated silver, the contrast is extraordinary — the warm gold seems to illuminate the depth of the stone, as if coaxing light from darkness itself.

AMBERRA's Curation Process for Color-Matched Sets

Creating a cohesive set — a necklace, earrings, and ring intended to be worn together — requires not only finding stones of compatible size and cut, but matching their color at a level of precision that photographs simply cannot convey. Our artisans examine each stone under natural Balinese daylight and again under candlelight, observing how the color shifts and deepens. Sets are assembled over weeks, sometimes months, as we wait for pieces that truly belong together.

This patient approach is fundamental to what AMBERRA creates. Color is not simply appearance — it is the record of a stone's geological biography. When you wear a matched set, you are carrying stones that shared something in their origin: the same forest, perhaps, the same mineral-rich soil, the same slow pressure of time. That coherence is worth waiting for.
