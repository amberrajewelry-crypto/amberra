From Ancient Forests to Bali: The Journey of Every AMBERRA Stone

January 2026
← Back to Journal

Every AMBERRA piece begins not in a workshop, but in deep geological time — in the thick canopy of a tropical forest that no longer exists, on an island that has not yet taken its present shape. The story of each stone spans approximately 40 million years, and understanding that story changes the way you hold one in your hand.

The Birth of Amber: 40 Million Years in the Making

Amber is not a mineral grown from the earth. It is the fossilized resin of ancient trees — most likely species related to the modern dipterocarp family that still dominates Southeast Asian rainforests today. During the Eocene epoch, the islands of the Indonesian archipelago were draped in forests of extraordinary density. When these trees were wounded — by insects, by fungal attack, by storm damage — they bled resin in prodigious quantities. This resin was the tree's immune response, sealing the wound and protecting against further decay.

In most cases, resin simply dried, degraded, and vanished. But in the right conditions — buried quickly under sediment, protected from oxygen, subjected to steady geological pressure and mild heat over millions of years — it underwent a process called polymerization. Volatile terpene compounds evaporated or transformed, and the resin hardened into the remarkably stable organic solid we call amber.

"A piece of Balinese amber is not merely old. It is a direct physical record of a living moment — a tree, a wound, a response — preserved with a fidelity that stone cannot match."

From Earth to Hand: Finding the Stones

Balinese amber is found in specific geological formations in Indonesia, typically in the alluvial deposits and sedimentary layers that were once ancient riverbeds and coastal shelves. Unlike the organized industrial mining of Baltic amber on the shores of the North Sea, the collection of Indonesian amber is largely an artisanal practice carried out by small family operations with generations of knowledge about where and how to look.

Raw amber arrives in rough nodules — irregular, often encrusted with sediment and oxidized surface material that gives little indication of what lies within. Experienced hands know how to read the weight and texture of a piece, how to judge from a small clean surface whether the interior will yield honey gold or deep cognac, whether the clarity will be exceptional or milky. This initial selection is itself a skill developed over lifetimes.

The Artisans of Ubud

AMBERRA is made in Ubud, the creative heart of Bali, by craftspeople who belong to a tradition of metalwork and stone-setting that is centuries old. Our workshop, operated under PT Amberra Group Creations, employs artisans whose families have worked in decorative arts for generations — carvers, polishers, silversmiths, and setters who understand their materials with the kind of intimacy that only comes from long practice.

Once raw amber arrives, it passes through careful stages. Outer crusts are removed by hand. Pieces are examined under natural light for clarity, color consistency, and the presence of inclusions — trapped bubbles, ancient plant matter, or the occasional prehistoric insect that makes a stone genuinely rare. Selected pieces are shaped on grinding wheels, moving through progressively finer grits until the surface becomes the warm, luminous curve that characterizes an AMBERRA stone. Final polishing is done by hand.

The Silver Setting

Every stone is then matched to its setting. Our silversmiths work in 925 sterling silver — an alloy of 92.5% pure silver and 7.5% copper, chosen for its malleability, durability, and the warm white gleam that complements honey amber so naturally. For our gold-plated collection, the sterling silver base is plated with 24k gold using a process that deposits a rich, warm layer over the entire piece.

Settings are fabricated by hand. Bezels are cut, shaped, and soldered under a torch flame. Each stone is tested in its setting before the bezel walls are pressed in to hold it — amber cannot be easily removed once set without risk of damage, so the fit must be perfect before the stone is secured.

The Sacred Blessing

Before any AMBERRA piece leaves our workshop, it receives a traditional Balinese blessing. This is not ceremony for ceremony's sake — it is an expression of the genuine belief, shared by our artisans, that objects made with care and intention carry that energy into the world. A Balinese Hindu priest consecrates each collection using prayers, holy water, and offerings drawn from the island's ritual calendar. For the people who make these pieces, this step is as essential as the soldering or the polishing. We honor that belief completely.

What arrives in your hands is the end of a very long journey: from a wound in an ancient tree, through millennia of geological transformation, through the careful work of artisans in Ubud, and through a ceremony of blessing and intention. We think that matters — and that it is part of what you feel when you hold an AMBERRA piece for the first time.
